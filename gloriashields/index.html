    Taught by Jenn de la Fuente, public relations professor at the University of Southern California.

    Here is a list of examples, resources and other fun stuff from the workshop. Feel free to pass along and share. If you have questions, email jdelafue@usc.edu.

    Social Media Storytelling Examples

    One big question that gets asked is "how can we repurpose content across different platforms?" An NPR station near and dear to me, WLRN in Miami, does a pretty good job with its social media engagement and storytelling and can be a great source of ideas. You can find them on Twitter and Instagram.

    Here are a few specific samples of their social media work:

    Tallahassee Takeover: How public health decisions get made in Florida

    • Twitter thread - This was a thread created to promote this particular podcast episode. It walks through some of the history of the public health system in the state and intersperses still photos and audiograms with the regular thread text.
    • Instagram post - This is a similar promo created for Instagram using similar language to what was in the Twitter thread. For the graphics, they opted to present it as a timeline in text, and used the caption to summarize the podcast episode (with a link in bio to listen).

    The one-year anniversary of the Surfside condo collapse

    • Twitter thread - A thread highlighting several main developments regarding the collapse: revisiting some of the people who were there and survived, or helped survivors; the $1 billion settlement for the families; the lasting impacts of the collapse and laws created to ensure better building safety in Florida. A great combo of straight up tweets, embeds, and linking back to previous stories written. It's a nicely crafted timeline that also has a solid narrative flow and jumps back and forth between the past and present well.

    Web Accessibility / Color Blindness Resources

    • Accessible Social Media - Fantastic tips and a checklist from accessibility advocate Alexa Heinrich on making your images and posts accessible across different social media platforms. You can also read about alt text for social media images and using icons and emojis in a more accessible fashion on her site.
    • Accessible Social Media checklist - A handy checklist to use for social media teams.
    • Emojipedia - Ever wonder what a screen reader would say if you posted an emoji? Here's where to find out. You can also see what an emoji looks like across different platforms and operating systems.
    • Writing better alt text
    • Memes are still largely inaccessible to the blind
    • Making Memes Accessible - a research paper about making memes accessible. Pretty academic language, but still some great tips and examples here.
    • Color Blindness Simulator - Ever wonder what the world looks like to someone who is color-blind? Want to check that your design has good contrast and still looks fine even to someone who can't see all its colors? Use this color-blindness checker to test!
    • Color contrast checker - Is your color combination (background color + text) difficult to read? Does it meet accessibility standards? This is a web-based contrast checker you can use.
    • Pika - If you're a Mac user, this is a free utility you can use to check contrast, and the eyedropper can pick any color out of any program you have up on your screen.

    Graphic / Photo Tools

    • Adobe Express - For iOS and Android. Make simple social graphics with photos and text.
    • The Noun Project - Royalty-free icons of all kinds of objects, drawn in all kinds of styles.
    • Google's Autodraw - If you don't have graphics folks or are a terrible artist and need simple icons, this is a great source. Scribble something and let Google try to guess what you're drawing. (You can download the resulting icons.)
    • Piktochart and Infogram - Two infographic generators. Can be embedded on a webpage or within most content platforms (like WordPress).
    • Canva - Another graphics creator.
    • Giphy Capture - Desktop app to create gifs (Mac only).
    • iFakeTextMessage - If you want to display a hypothetical text message conversation, you can create a static image using this tool.
    • Texting Story Chat Maker - If you want to simulate a text conversation and make it a video, you can use this tool. Free, with some extra features unlocked if you pay. For both iPhone and Android.

    Video Tools

    • Adobe Rush - Quick editing software that works on your phone, tablet, or desktop. Like a stripped-down (but still powerful) version of Adobe Premiere. Available for Mac, Windows, iOS, and Android.
    • DoubleTake by Filmic Pro - Free app for iOS that allows for simultaneous filming through the iPhone's multiple lenses (for example, you can get wide-angle and portrait lens footage at the same time and switch between those views instantly, or film from the back camera and selfie cameras at the same time.

    Audio Tools

    • Anchor - Mobile app that allows you to create your own radio show quickly using just a mobile phone. Record quick news briefs, allow people to respond and call in, post your clips to social media quickly.
    • SoundCloud - Great place to upload any audio stories. Embeddable just about anywhere.
    • SoundCite - Tool to create audio that you can embed inline into a story. For instance, if you're writing about a protest, you can add some natural sound into your story using SoundCite.
    • Headliner - A way to create visually compelling audio clips with custom backgrounds and waveforms. Can be embedded on Twitter and Facebook, and great for audio teasers.

    Layout Tools (for standalone special projects, for example)

    • Adobe Express Pages - A quick way to create a compelling page layout without HTML. Drag and drop! Can be embedded into a blank HTML page and is mobile friendly.
    • Carrd - Another one-page builder. Free.
    • Storyform - A way to create responsive story layouts. More of a magazine style, where you swipe from page to page.
    • Ceros - Similar to Adobe Spark Pages; you can create a one-page scrolling story/experience. A bit more robust than Adobe Spark Pages. Sample here from Legacy High School.
    • Playbuzz - Another tool for creating slide-like, full-page stories that advance from one window to another. Embeddable in pages! Also great for creating quizzes.

    360 Tools

    • Thinglink - Create 360 experiences that can be annotated. For a student example, check out this story and scroll to the section about "Gram's Room."
    • RoundMe - Another 360 experience creator. Available as a mobile app as well.

    Other Online Tools

    • StoryMap JS - Create a curated map with clickable points. You can embed different kinds of media into the map. Great if you have a story tied to geography or various points in your town. Embeddable into a webpage or most content management systems.
    • Timeline JS - Create an interactive timeline with all sorts of media. Embeddable into a webpage or most content management systems.
    • Chatfuel - If you want to experiment with creating a chatbot on Facebook Messenger, this is a great place to start. Requires no code.
    • MailChimp - Newsletters aren't just for brands! They're a great way to keep readers informed, reach out to alumni, and just keep people up to date on your great content. If you're looking to start small and start free, MailChimp is a terrific place to start. Super simple to use and configure, and easy to embed the sign up form on pages or WordPress.
    • Medium - Need to just publish quickly and don't want to fiddle with templates or figure out how to start up a WordPress or SquareSpace site? Use Medium. Write and publish right away. It'll look a bit vanilla, but you can quickly share and distribute your content, and Medium is a robust publishing network. Great if you don't have a lot of web resources.
